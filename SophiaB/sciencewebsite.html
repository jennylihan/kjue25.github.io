Frequently asked questions:

What is the Passion fruit's scientific name?
The Passion fruit's scientific name is Passiflora edulis. You can also tell if you know its genus and species. Then you capitalize the first letter of the genus and lowercase the first letter of the species. In this case the genus is Passiflora (notice the first letter capitalized), and the species is edulis (once again noticing the lowercase letter).

What does the passion fruit look like?
The passion fruit is a round exotic fruit with a slightly tart taste. It has these flavorful seeds on the inside coated in passion fruit juice.

Where does the passion fruit come from?
The passion fruit originally came from the country of Brazil, but it is now grown in many different parts of the world.

When can I get the passion fruit?
You can get the passion fruit at any time! The passion fruits are grown and harvested year-round, so you won't have to wait to try this delicious delicacy.
