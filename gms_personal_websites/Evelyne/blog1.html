Beluga Whales

Scientific Taxonomy

Kingdom: Animalia

Phylum: Chordata

Class: Mammalia

Order: Artiodactyla

Infraorder: Cetacea

Family: Monodontidae

Genus: Delphinapterus

Species: D. leucas

Beluga Whales Summary:

The beluga whales are a fascinating beautiful creature in the arctic. They use high pitched sounds and clicks for communication. And locate other animals using Echolocation, other animals that use this method of communication include bats, dolphins & shrews. They are carnivores & mammals. They eat crabs, octopus, squid, clams, fish and more. They weigh 1-1.5 tons which is 2000-3000 pounds & they are 13-20 feet long. They live in pods with other Belugas and they are social animals. Arctic Belugas migrate south to not get trapped underwater by ice (which will kill them). Beluga Whales are sometimes hunted by indigenous people and fisherman (commercial). Beluga’s average life span is 35-50 years. They are a magnificent species, which we should protect.

Fun Facts:

-Beluga whales have teeth but don’t chew their food

-Beluga’s unique color of white/grey is why they are easy to recognize/spot

-Beluga’s are related to the Narwhal, they are in the same family (Monodontidae)

-Beluga’s come from the Russian word for white, “bielo” or “belyy”.
