Science- Axolotls (A. mexicanum)

By: Karina

What does it look like?

Axolotl can be black, mottled brown, albino,or white. This animal looks like a tadpole but has limbs, a dorsal fin, and a pair of fuzzy looking gills coming out of its head.

Axolotl Status

Kingdom: Animalia
Domain: Eukaryote
Phylum: Chordata
Class: Amphibia
Order: Urodela
Family: Ambystomatidae
Genus: Ambystoma

Fun Facts



  • Like other salamanders, Axolotl have the ability to regrow body organs, lost limbs, even the spinal cord! Every time one part gets detached or crushed, a replacement will grow perfectly. However, axolotl can only regrow the same limb about five times.

  • Male axolotls have wide heads and no eyelids, unlike the female axolotl.

  • The breeding process for axolotl is a walz.

  • A mythological connector to the Axolotl is Xolotl, a dog-headed Aztec God. In the stories, Xolotl was scared of being sacrificed to the sun as Aztec gods had to do, and transformed into an Axolotl to hide.

  • Axolotl's closest relative is a tiger salamander.

  • As soon as Axolotl are hatched, they have no parenting, and are independant.

  • Axolotls live in Mexico, and were once eaten for nutrition. However, it is now a popular pet to have in Japan.

  • Toothless from How to Train Your Dragon was inspired to look like an Axolotl.

  • There are about 700 to 1,200 axolotls left in the wild due to habitat loss. By donating to charities, scientists will breed more axolotl to prevent it from going extinct.
