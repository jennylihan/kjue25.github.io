A bit about Jaguars:

The jaguar (Panthera onca) is a large felid species and the only extant member of the genus Panthera native to the Americas. The Jaguar is the third largest cat in the world! It's coat is generally tan, but ranges to reddish-brown for most of the body. Look below to see the Jaguar's full taxonomic classifacation:

Why Jaguars are so Important

"Jaguars are the top predators in their environment, so they play an important role in controlling the populations of other species. This helps keep a balance in the food chain, and a healthy environment. By protecting jaguars and the places where they live, we’re also helping to look after other wildlife – of which there are a lot of in the Amazon and Pantanal – as well as the people who live and work around there." (Credit: wwf.org)
