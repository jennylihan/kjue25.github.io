Problem Statement:

Brown vs. Board of Education ruled out school segregation 64 years ago. Yet, separate and unequal still exists within the school system.

In 1954, communities were segregated not just by schools but by race and socioeconomic status by law. Today, this de facto segregation is the result of decades of millions of individual homeowners' decisions to select neighborhoods. School choice, whether we accept it or not, has wide-reaching effects on educational equity.

Racial resegregation within public schools was the top most underreported story in Time magazine (*). Consider this:

Today, African-American and Latino youth are more segregated than they were in this year that MLK Jr. died (*).

It may be the history of America, but it is also the future we will create.

Target Audience:

Any stressed out new parent or college student.

Not all parents have the privilege of having multiple schooling options for their child. Those who do face a daunting decision, rife with tradeoffs. This tool will help parents understand where their values lie, prioritize what factors determine a "good" school in their eyes, and reflect on how their decisions affect society.

College students without children should also be reflecting on these social issues as they progress through the education system. Sociospatial segregation is a phenomenon that not only affects all of us but also is a force that we affect, every time we decide where to find a place to live, work, consume, etc. In particular, today's college students and graduates will likely be those who have the power and privilege to choose schools for their children in the future. We will have to wrestle with our values and priorities. Our project centers around understanding where those values lie for each one of us.Through user testing, we found that college students our age responded very positively to this explorable.
